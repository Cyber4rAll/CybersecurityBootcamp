Incident Response, Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

Planning for the Unexpected

When we're talking about IR, BC, and DR, we're focusing on availability, which is accomplished through these concepts.

  • Incident Response (IR) plan: responds to unexpected changes in operating conditions to keep the business operating.
  • Business Continuity (BC) plan: enables the business to continue operating throughout the crisis.
  • Disaster Recovery (DR) plan: is activated to help the business to return to normal operations as quickly as possible, if Incident Response and Business Continuity plans fail.

Module 1: Incident Terminology

Key Terms (1/2)

Breach: The loss of control, compromise, unauthorized disclosure, unauthorized acquisition, or any similar occurrence where: a person other than an authorized user accesses or potentially accesses personally identifiable information.

Event: Any observable occurrence in a network or system.

Exploit: A particular attack. It is named this way because these attacks exploit system vulnerabilities.

Incident: An event that actually or potentially jeopardizes the confidentiality, integrity or availability of an information system.

Module 1: Incident Terminology

Key Terms (2/2)

Intrusion: A security event in which an intruder gains, or attempts to gain, access to a system without authorization.

Threat: Any circumstance or event with the potential to adversely impact organizational operations through unauthorized access, destruction, disclosure, etc.

Vulnerability: Weakness in a system that could be exploited by a threat source.

Zero Day: A previously unknown system vulnerability with the potential of exploitation without risk of detection or prevention.

The Goal of Incident Response

The priority of any incident response is to protect life, health, and safety. When any decision related to priorities is to be made, always choose safety first.

The primary goal is to be prepared. Preparation requires having a policy and a response plan that will lead the organization through the crisis. Every organization must have an incident response plan that will help preserve business viability and survival.

Components of the IR Plan

Preparation

The incident response policy should reference an incident response plan that all employees will follow.

  • Develop a policy approved by management.
  • Identify critical data and systems, single points of failure.
  • Train staff on incident response; Implement an incident response team.
  • Plan the coordination of communication between stakeholders.

Components of the IR Plan

Response Stages

  • Detection and Analysis: Monitor all possible attack vectors; Analyze incident using known data and threat intelligence.
  • Containment, Eradication, and Recovery: Gather evidence; Choose an appropriate containment strategy; Isolate the attack.
  • Post-incident Activity: Identify evidence that may need to be retained. Document lessons learned.

Incident Response Team

Key Members & Responsibilities

A cross-functional group of individuals to represent various areas of the organization.

  • Senior management, information security, legal, public affairs, and engineering representatives.
  • Responsibilities include: determining damage, identifying compromised information, implementing recovery procedures, and supervising additional security measures.

Module 2: Business Continuity

The Importance of Business Continuity

The intent of a business continuity plan is to sustain business operations while recovering from a significant disruption.

A key part of the plan is communication. Management must be included. We need to have at hand the critical contact numbers for the supply chain, as well as law enforcement and other sites outside of the facility.

Components of a BCP

Business continuity planning (BCP) is the proactive development of procedures to restore business operations after a disaster.

  • List of BCP team members and contact methods.
  • Immediate response procedures and checklists.
  • Notification systems and call trees.
  • Guidance for management and how/when to enact the plan.
  • Contact numbers for critical members of the supply chain.

Module 3: Disaster Recovery

The Goal of Disaster Recovery

Disaster recovery planning steps in where BC leaves off. The DRP guides actions until the end goal is reached—which is to see the business restored to full last-known reliable operations.

Disaster recovery refers specifically to restoring the information technology and communications services and systems needed by an organization.

Components of a DRP

  • Executive summary providing a high-level overview.
  • Department-specific plans and technical guides for IT personnel.
  • Full copies of the plan for critical team members.
  • Checklists for certain individuals.
  • Public relations should be a member of the DRP.
  • IT Personnel are primarily responsible for the disaster recovery team.
